TOP 3 TIPS

  1. Find a spot with the right light

    When deciding where to place a plant, keep in mind that south-facing windows receive much more light than north-facing ones. With east- or west-facing windows, observe the amounts of morning or afternoon sun they get before putting plants near them.

  2. Read the newspaper to your plants

    Seriously. This can benefit them in two ways. One, the carbon dioxide in your breath may energize their gas exchange cycles. Two, if there’s enough natural light for you to read by, you’ll know that the plants are getting the minimum amount of light needed for good growth.

  3. Use ice cubes to water

    Place them around the soil, but not touching the stem. The ice will melt slowly, releasing water gradually and evenly into the soil.
